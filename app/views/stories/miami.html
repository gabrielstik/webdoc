Wynwood is a neighborhood in Miami known internationally to house some of the world's finest urban artwork. It became the artistic center of south Florida by having more than 70 galleries and museums in addition to the impressive outdoor works of art. But 50 years ago, Wynwood wasn’t what we know nowadays.

1950's
The now famous district of Wynwood is not what it used to be. Once called Little San Juan after being Miami’s culture center for Puerto Ricans that arrived during the 1950s. Roward the 1980s, Wynwood became one of Miami’s poorest neighborhoods and became one of the most dangerous area, you could not find any tourist in the street. The change took place when artists settled in the neighborhood, creating gallery in old warehouse in order to bring life to this gloomy district.
From ghetto
to gallery
Wynwood went from a dangerous ghetto to a popular tourist attraction. The renewal of Wynwood is due to the artist who settled there but specially to Tony Goldman, a real estate developer who invested in the neighborhood and asked to some artists to express themselves on the walls of his buildings. The famous Wynwood walls are created in 2009, walls that became quickly a huge white canvas for every artist and become the most instagrammed place of Miami.

You can call Wynwood a « underground » neighborhood but will it stay that way? Who says growth, tourism and mass of new places says rising prices and a super important gentrification. The result of the raising price might be that only major brands will be able to pay the rent in Wynwood. With that being said, for some people, Wynwood might lose its soul.
