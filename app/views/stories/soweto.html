Soweto is a township in the west of Johannesburg. Its name come from the contraction of South West Township. Formerly a separate city, it is now considered as a suburb of Joburg. Soweto's population is overwhelmingly black. All eleven of the country's official languages are spoken, and the main linguistic groups are Zulu, Sotho, Tswana.

1976
Soweto came to the world's attention on 16 June 1976 with the Soweto Uprising, when mass protests erupted over the government's policy to enforce education in Afrikaans rather than their native language. The impact of the Soweto protests reverberated through the country and across the world. In their aftermath, economic and cultural sanctions were introduced from abroad. Political activists left the country to train for guerrilla resistance.

Dancing in
the streets

Especially one : the street dancers, like Skeleton Pantsula Crew. Their routine is a hybrid of contortion, magic tricks and pantsula, a style of dancing that developed in South African townships in the ‘70s and has since become part of the national culture. The beauty in pantsula is : many forms of it exists and it’s to the dancer liking to add his own personal move to make it unique.
